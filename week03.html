This week examined how users interact with systems through different interaction styles. Interaction styles define the communication between users and computers.

Dix et al. identify several common interaction styles, each with its own advantages and limitations depending on the context of use (Dix et al., Chapter 4).

Interaction Styles

  • Command-line interaction
  • Menu-based systems
  • Form-based interaction
  • Direct manipulation interfaces

Choosing an appropriate interaction style is essential for usability and depends on user expertise and task complexity.
