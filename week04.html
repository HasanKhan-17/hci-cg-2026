Overview

Week 04 emphasized understanding user errors and how interface design can prevent or mitigate them.

Dix et al. categorize errors as slips (execution errors) and mistakes (planning errors) (Chapter 8). Effective feedback and recovery mechanisms improve usability.

Key Learning Points

  • Slips: unintended actions during task execution
  • Mistakes: incorrect intentions or goals
  • Feedback mechanisms for error correction
  • Designing for recoverability and learnability

Reflection

By analyzing errors, we can identify design flaws and improve system usability. Providing clear instructions and immediate feedback reduces both slips and mistakes.
