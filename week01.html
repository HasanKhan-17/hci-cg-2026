Week 01 – Introduction to Human–Computer Interaction

The first week introduced the field of Human–Computer Interaction and its importance in modern computing. HCI is concerned with making interactive systems usable, efficient, and satisfying for users.

Dix et al. describe HCI as a multidisciplinary field that combines computer science, psychology, ergonomics, and design to improve the interaction between humans and computers (Dix et al., Chapter 1).

Key Concepts

  • Definition and scope of HCI
  • Importance of usability
  • User-centered system design

This week emphasized that poor interface design can lead to errors and frustration, while good design enhances productivity and user experience.
