Week 01 – Introduction to HCI

Overview

Week 01 introduced the field of Human–Computer Interaction, explaining why usability and user-centered design are critical for modern systems.

According to Dix et al., HCI focuses on designing systems that fit human cognitive and perceptual abilities while minimizing errors (Chapter 1).

Key Learning Points

  • Definition and scope of HCI
  • Importance of usability and user satisfaction
  • User-centered design approach
  • Multidisciplinary nature of HCI: psychology, design, and computer science

Reflection

Understanding the basics of HCI helps in framing future lab work, as it emphasizes why design decisions should be based on user needs rather than just technical considerations.
