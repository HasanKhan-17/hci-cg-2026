This week focused on understanding the human component of interactive systems. Human abilities and limitations strongly influence how systems should be designed.

According to Dix et al., factors such as perception, attention, memory, and motor skills play a critical role in interaction design (Dix et al., Chapter 2).

Key Concepts

  • Human perception and sensory systems
  • Short-term and long-term memory
  • Cognitive processing and attention

Designers must account for these factors to reduce cognitive load and ensure that interfaces align with natural human behavior.
