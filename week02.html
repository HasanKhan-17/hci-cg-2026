Overview

Week 02 focused on human capabilities and limitations that influence system design. This includes perception, memory, attention, and motor skills.

Dix et al. highlight that understanding these factors allows designers to reduce cognitive load and create interfaces that align with natural human behavior (Chapter 2).

Key Learning Points

  • Human perception and sensory processing
  • Memory: short-term, long-term, and working memory
  • Attention: selective, divided, and sustained
  • Motor skills and response time considerations

Reflection

Designers must consider human limitations to prevent errors and frustration. For example, too many simultaneous options may overwhelm short-term memory, while poorly labeled buttons can confuse perception.
