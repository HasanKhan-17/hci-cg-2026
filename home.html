Introduction

This portfolio documents my learning and practical work for the Human–Computer Interaction (HCI) course. It is structured into weekly highlights, lab observations, and interaction model analysis. The goal is to reflect both theoretical and practical aspects of HCI while keeping the original repository intact.

Human–Computer Interaction studies the design, evaluation, and implementation of interactive systems that support human use. It combines knowledge from computer science, psychology, and design to create usable and efficient systems (Dix et al., Chapter 1).

This portfolio focuses on:

  • Weekly summaries and reflections (Weeks 01–04)
  • Analysis of common user errors
  • Interaction models: Norman, GOMS, and KLM
  • Application of HCI principles to interface evaluation

Reference

Dix, A., Finlay, J., Abowd, G. D., & Beale, R.
Human–Computer Interaction, Pearson Education.
